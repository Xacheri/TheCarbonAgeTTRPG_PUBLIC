**Humans** — Play as **Human**. Baseline Earthborn humanity; still the overwhelming majority of the sentient population.

**Elves** — Play as **[[Starfolk]]**. A rare **mutant human** lineage with a talent for the arcane. Supposedly originating from an Alien bloodline, many doubt whether they even exist. Most can pass as human with minor tells (pale iridescence in the eyes, atypical bone structure).**

**Half-Orcs** — Play as **[[Chem-Mutant]]**. A post-bombing **industrial-exposure mutant** lineage originating in Japan: hypertrophic musculature, adrenal overexpression, and heightened aggression response tied to prolonged **[Carbodiesel](../Lore/Petrochems/Carbodiesel.md)** / **[Hydromethane](../Lore/Petrochems/Hydromethane.md)** burn fumes and chemical runoff. Hard to miss, hard to “blend,” and often stereotyped as raiders even when they aren’t.

**Dwarves** — Play as **[[Cinderborn]]**. A stocky, hardy **subterranean-adapted mutant human** lineage descended from long-term bunker and mine populations. Dense bone, high pain tolerance, soot-stained skin that doesn’t sunburn so much as _char_, and lungs adapted to particulates. Renowned mechanics and fabricators of petro-chem vehicles and heavy equipment; many are brilliant builders but (by culture, not biology) often prefer wrenching to piloting. Can pass as human at a glance, but the build and skin give them away. 

**Tiefling** — Play as a **[[Cursed]]**. A rare **post-Calamity genetic deviation**: horn growth, tails, exotic skin color, heat-resistant dermis patches, and an innate sensitivity to the Arcane that began appearing after the magnetic incidents during the **[Calamity](../Lore/Historical%20Ledger/Calamity%20(The%20Calamity).md)**. Some communities treat them as omens; other regard them with pity and disgust.

**Gnomes** — Play as **[[Wireling]]**. A small, sharp-featured **microstature mutant human** lineage linked to generations of industrial endocrine disruption, heavy metal exposure, and selective survival in dense factory cities. Renowned for obsessive tinkering, improvised gadgets, and “scrap-born” engineering culture. Usually travel as indispensable mechanics, riggers, or signal techs. 

**Halflings** — Play as **[[Halfling]]**. A smaller-than-average **human lineage** that began appearing more commonly in the late 1970s. Widely accepted as human; theories range from pollutant-linked growth stunting to adaptive miniaturization for scarcity-era living. Some factions claim they’re the first true “post-industrial” human phenotype.
