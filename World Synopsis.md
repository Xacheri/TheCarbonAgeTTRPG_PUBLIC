Welcome to Earth's Carbon Age.

The year is [2004](Historical%20Ledger/2004.md), 59 years after the tragedy of [Hiroshima](Hiroshima.md) in [1945](Historical%20Ledger/1945.md), when the U.S. revealed to the world the horror of the [Supermassive Yield Carbo-Diesel Bomb](Supermassive%20Yield%20Carbo-Diesel%20Bomb.md), and ushered in a new era of energy, weapons, and fossil fuel production.

Though the government that was once the [United States](United%20States.md) dissolved after eastern coastline slipped off the side of North America in [1984](Historical%20Ledger/1984.md), that scarred city in Japan is still aflame. Even today, all the lands which have been touched by [Carbodiesel](Petrochems/Carbodiesel.md) weapons still burn, casting carbon into the atmosphere as it scorches every atom of the city over and over again.

Soon after the incident in [Hiroshima](Hiroshima.md), other nations revealed their hands too. [China](China.md) revealed it's discovery of [Hydromethane](Petrochems/Hydromethane.md), a low-density high-yield high-temperature fuel that quickly propelled the country to the front of the space race and military aviation. The fuel was also rumored to have some some high-tech weapons applications. 

Unsurprisingly, the [USSR](USSR.md) had worked on it's own fuel, [Cryoethyline](Petrochems/Cryoethyline.md) was a highly affordable and versatile fuel developed in partnership between the Soviet Union's agricultural and petroleum sectors. The renewable petrochem acted both as an efficient, but low power, combustion fuel and as an unparalleled coolant under high pressures. This discovery boosted the Soviet Union out of the scarcity of it's early era, and supported their population of the [Artic Region](Artic%20Region.md).

While the discovery of these petrochems propelled humanity into a technological golden age, the accelerated production of goods and violence using these chemicals accelerated Earth's climate change faster than humanity's scientists could possibly react. 

In the year [1984](Historical%20Ledger/1984.md), great storms known as [[Super-Typhoons]] which had in the prior few years only raged within the world's oceans, began to cycle their way ashore. The storms accompanied massive geological disturbances, wrought by decades of intense drilling, in a catastrophe that this event known as the [Calamity](Historical%20Ledger/Calamity%20(The%20Calamity).md). Major governments of the world collapsed within a span of months as their capital cities succumbed to the rising sea levels, resource shortages, and civil violence. 

Though decades have passed since the [Calamity](Historical%20Ledger/Calamity%20(The%20Calamity).md), and most of the truly elderly have succumbed to the elements of this new world, many still remember a time before the world went to hell.
