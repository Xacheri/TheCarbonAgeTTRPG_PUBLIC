## What was the world like before it ended?
The critical divergence of this world from the world we live in today is that instead of humanity discovering the power of the Atom near the end of World War II, they unlocked secrets to creating extremely powerful, and extremely pollutant, fossil fuels. Everyday cars and trucks were larger in this world, fueled by these cheap and powerful **petrochems**, but these were nothing compared to the massive machines of war militaries were able to build and wield against each other. 

The Soviet Union was much more successful, as their own particularly cheap **petrochem** helped pull the nation from resource scarcity and the **Red Scare** was stronger than ever among Americans. The Soviet Union was able to populate much more of the Artic, taking a small amount of territory from Canadians. 

China won the Space Race hands-down. They developed their superfuel with space aspirations in mind, rather than aspirations of war like the US. They were the world leaders in spacecraft and aircraft alike.

The United States was a country that loved freedom, fossil fuels, and killing communists. Their petrochem was excellent for creating weapons and vehicles of war as well as powering their ever growing custom car culture. 

Governments around the world ignored the signs of the worsening climate claiming it was a natural process that the Earth had always gone through while remodeling their bathrooms with money from the oil lobby which had become more powerful than ever. Major mutations became commonplace and people got sick at an increasing rapid pace until eventually, Mother Earth got revenge in the form of [Calamity (The Calamity)](Calamity%20%28The%20Calamity%29.md).

## How did the world end?
The world ended in 1984 as a result of [Calamity (The Calamity)](Calamity%20%28The%20Calamity%29.md), which was a chain of extreme natural disasters, and society's inability to react to these disasters, in this order:

- **July -** *Super-Typhoons Move Inland* Large storms that had dominated the ocean in recent years, cutting off Atlantic supply chains, began to move inland. In the US, cities in the East Coast and parts of the Midwest are blown away by the powerful storms.
- **July** *- Ronald Reagan Bombs China* Ronald Reagan during the disaster, absolutely coked out of his mind, launches [[Supermassive Yield Carbo-Diesel Bomb]]s at China in retaliation for, what he believed, was a massive weather machine attack on the East Coast. The U.S. begins dissolving due to incompetent governance and extreme storm damage to critical national infrastructure. Chinese population centers are submerged in [Carbodiesel](Petrochems/Carbodiesel.md) fire.
- **August** *- Supercharged Weather Disasters Sweep The Globe* Many smaller governments succumb to the intense stress and chaos of intense, out-of-season, repetitive weather disasters.
- **September** *- Earth's Magnetic Poles Flip* Electronics fry across the globe, requiring repair efforts beyond the general capabilities of the remaining supply chain. The first [Starfolk](Starfolk.md) mutations appear in the Artic. Humans start accessing [[The Flux]] for the first time.
- **September** *- Rapid Thawing of Permafrost* Sea levels rise, and ancient diseases like the [The Black Death](Diseases/The%20Black%20Death.md) are revived. World populations thin as they scramble for the remaining medicine.
- **November** - *Cursed Mutagen devastates the global population* A contagious mutagen spread through the world like a wildfire, killing around 60 percent of the remaining population. The remaining people on earth are humans who never caught the disease and their descendants, [Cursed](Cursed.md) mutants descended from humans who survived the disease, and other mutants who would have been immune.
- **February 1985** - *World Governments are no more* Most world governments have collapsed or transformed completely. Notably the [[Post-Canadian Union]] has done well at downsizing itself and focusing on protecting the things important to post-apocalyptic life

## What is the world like now?
The Carbon Age setting takes place in the early 2000s, about 20 years after The Calamity.

Urban centers which were devastated by the natural disasters and lawlessness of The Calamity have had their husks resettled by the sparse remnants of humanity. Tribes of raiders hold the most territory in these places, levying violence to get their way. Small communities make their homes in secured sectors and buildings. Destroyed and debris-covered roads often make travel difficult for large land vehicles in these areas.

Rural areas are generally rather sparse of humans, but the ones who live there rely on jury-rigged fossil-fuel powered vehicles and weaponry to get around and defend themselves. Wild animals have also mutated from the effects of the past's favorite chemicals of the week, and present a much more dangerous threat, calling into question the idea that humans are on top of the food chain.

Individuals who were adults during The Calamity are now in their 40s and 50s if they've managed to survive this long. Some of those who survived did so in luxury emergency bunkers, only emerging once they knew it was safe while some lived to watch their friends and families die from storms, disease, and mutation.

Most of the younger folk do not remember a time before the world was like this, and only remember hearing the stories and seeing the pictures. Mutants are commonplace, even more-so than before The Calamity, but many remaining Humans are still just as suspicious of them as they were before. 

Small governments and communities have formed throughout the ruins of cities and towns. Law and Order is still a distant dream for most places though. Even people who were once considered sane fight and kill one another over food, medicine, weaponry, and the occasional piece of working technology.

The world now is grim, but some hope remains in the hands of those heroes and community members who seek to adapt the values of honesty, community, and honor to a world built on desperation, destruction, and greed.

## Is there magic in the world?
Yes!

During The Calamity, there was an event that flipped Earth's magnetic poles, frying nearly all electronics and causing widespread havoc. An effect of this, however, is that some individuals began to be able to access a force known as [[The Flux]]. Individuals who are gifted with the ability report nightmares and of curses that comes along with their gift, while those who are studied in the usage of [[The Flux]] all disagree nonsensically about the nature and science of [[The Flux]].

Divine Magic also made a resurgence, as it had in ancient times. Not everyone believes in these abilities, but most find it hard to deny when their gunshot wound has been healed by the prayers of a heavily armed chaplain. 

## Are there guns?
Yes!

Guns are standard 5E rule modern guns on the tabletop. This means they deal high damage numbers, and combat is faster and more lethal especially at earlier levels. 

There are some guns made using **petrochems** that are even stronger and have special abilities, doing damage more similar to the Futuristic Weapons in the DMG.
