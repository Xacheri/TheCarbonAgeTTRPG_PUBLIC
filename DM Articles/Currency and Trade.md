> Trade in the Carbon Age is defined by two things: Bullets and Barter. For smaller purchases, Bullets are used as a common currency, since there is virtually nobody who doesn't see their value. For larger purchases, individuals find that bartering small but valuable items, like jewelry or weapons, allows them to spare their backs the weight of all the bullets.

## Bullet Currency
Anywhere you can shop, you can spend Bullets. For currency purposes, the type of ammunition does not matter.

Every bullet is worth 2sp if compared to PHB currency. This makes 10 bullets worth 2gp, weighing 1lb. 

When paying out in bullets, shopkeepers will use their cheapest option available, typically 9mm pistol rounds. Change is not made if amounts are less than 2sp.
## Barter System
Most traders will also accept barters. Not all traders will place the same value on an item, or to the same customer. Relationships with the trader, trader's relevance to the item, as well as haggling contributes to the value appraised for an item.
