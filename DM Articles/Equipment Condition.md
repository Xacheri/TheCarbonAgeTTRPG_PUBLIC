## The Problem with Traditional 5E Condition
Often, in DND 5E, Equipment Condition is tied to an item's HP, and an Item takes HP damage as it is used. This system has 2 main issues:
1. Players have to track HP on all items. Players have to find HP values for every item. DM has to create HP values for many items. 
2. Equipment breakage becomes very predictable, and formulaic upkeep would prevent the danger of equipment breakage from affecting the game beyond creating that upkeep.

In our game, we will use a system of ***Item Condition*** and ***Condition Saves*** to add in the survival component of equipment breakage without building a spreadsheet simulator. 
## Item Condition
Every item in the game has a condition. If not specified, it can be assumed that an item which is new to the party is in **Decent** condition. A failed **Condition Save** lowers condition by 1 tier, and a successful **Repair** increases condition, usually by 1 tier. The skill used to repair an item depends on the item and the situation it is repaired in. Lower condition levels have downsides to use, and the *Broken* condition makes an item unusable and not easily repaired

| Tier | Condition      | Effects                                                                                                                                |
| ---- | -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0    | Broken         | The item is unusable and requires an expert craftsman to repair                                                                        |
| 1    | Malfunctioning | The item is difficult to use for it's intended purpose. Rolls related to using this item are made with **Disadvantage**                |
| 2    | Poor           | This item is in poor condition, and has lost most of it's resell value. Sells for 1/2 the listed value of the item.                    |
| 3    | Decent         | This item is in decent condition. Most loot is found at this condition level.                                                          |
| 4    | Good           | This item is in good condition. Most items are sold at this condition level.                                                           |
| 5    | New            | This item looks like it was freshly manufactured! Items like this are rare these days. Add 1d4 to D20 rolls made related to this item. |

## Condition Saves
A condition save is a flat **DC 10** save made at the request of the DM after an action that may potentially reduce the condition of an item (e.g. using a tent in the rain, critically failing an attack, leaving your gun in the mud).

If you are proficient in the skill which is related to the save (ask the DM if they do not specify. Not all Condition Saves have a related skill), you may take this save with **Advantage**.

If the Condition Save fails, the item will lose one tier of condition. If it succeeds, the item will not lose any condition. On a critical success, a player receives **Inspiration**. On a critical failure, the **item is destroyed**.
