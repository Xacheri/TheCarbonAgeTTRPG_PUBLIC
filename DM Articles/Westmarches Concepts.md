>*Our campaign is a post-apocalyptic survival/exploration Westmarches hexcrawl. That's a lot of words, so here is a quick breakdown of those ideas:*

## Westmarches Campaign
A westmarches campaign has a few key elements:
- Sandbox worlds and stories
- Drop-in, Drop-out session planning
- Players decide what they would like to do each session, and are free to toss a quest in the trash without derailing any sort of main story

It got it's name from an old campaign that took place in a world called the Westmarches, where the party every session decided what they wanted to do, adventuring west to do it. There's nothing really in the name that describes the type of game it is.

## Sandbox
Westmarches campaigns are sandboxes.
That means they are:
- **Exploration Driven** - Players explore the world which drives the gameplay which drives the story
- **Non-Linear Plots** - There is no pre-written, linear, plot to follow. The story emerges from player interaction with the world. 
- **Reactive Worlds** - Rather than the players making actions that move them along an unchanging story, the player actions, or inactions, cause the world to change and evolve alongside them

It does not mean they are:
- **Questless** - Sandboxes have quests! While there is no one "main questline," plenty of people in the world have tasks that need doing, dungeons that need exploring, and political plots that need thwarting.
- **Pointless** - Your actions have consequences! The players can choose to be a force for good, or evil, and influence the world in that way, causing new threats and opportunities to arise.
- **Grindy / Numbers-crunchy** - While this campaign does have a lot of survival elements that add a certain amount of numbers-crunch, sandboxes don't have to! You simply need mechanics that allow travel and create urgency to travel. 

## Hexcrawl
A Hexcrawl describes a way to run a player-driven exploration campaign by allowing them to explore the world by travelling throughout a pre-made hexmap.

Each hex will have a varying level of interesting things within them, from single-dice-roll encounters to dungeons to settlements, every hex has some sort of challenge within it to engage players.

Use of the hexmap encourages travel mechanics, and the necessary passage of time to use travel mechanics encourages survival mechanics. 

## Drop in, Drop Out
Perhaps the core feature of Westmarches-style campaigns is the ability to drop in or out of the game as needed. In Westmarches game, there is no obligation to attend every session. This enables the DM to run a game at a consistent time, with a varying roster of players.

Some concessions need to be made to enable this:
- Perfect party comps are impossible, when the party is not static
- The party must have a Home Base, where they can start every session.

## "Home" Base
The Home Base in our Westmarches campaign is the party's Camp, unless otherwise specified. It is assumed that all players who are not in session, have their characters at the Camp. Camp is located in a relatively safe place in whichever hex the party last long rested, should one exist.

The Camp moves with the party, in whatever hex they are, and characters separated from the party have the ability to find the camp, after a set of skill rolls and saves with potential consequences.

Alternatively, if the Party finds a property or properties that they would like to use instead, the Home Base can be at one of those properties.
