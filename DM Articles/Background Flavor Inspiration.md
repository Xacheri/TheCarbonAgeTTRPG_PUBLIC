You can use most base-game 5e backgrounds almost unchanged besides adjusting some flavor.
### Acolyte
Think like a Mad-Max preacher with a giant mobile chapel, or an Old World priest trying to keep the Catholic Faith alive. Divine Magic manifests through sincere belief, prayer, and devotion.

**Example:**  
A former Sunday school teacher who now travels with a shotgun and a flock of refugees.
### Criminal
Crime is the #1 most lucrative occupation in the post apocalypse, and comes in as many flavors of smuggling, drug running, stealing, murdering, and con artistry as it did before the world went sideways.

**Example:**  
A Chem-Mutant who used to run contraband fuel and is trying to go straight.

### Folk Hero
The person who held a block together during flood season, fought off raiders, repaired the settlement pump, or led people out of a burning bunker. Some people still do good and brave things, you know.

**Example:**  
A humble Halfling farmer who saved their small town during a mutated animal attack near the beginning of the Calamity.

### Guild Artisan
Like a craftsman in general with decentralized connections to other people who do their craft.

**Example:**  
A Cinderborn welder who builds armor for vehicles and has connections with mechanics in area through a local mesh network set up with rare repaired electronics.
### Hermit
It shouldn't be hard to use your imagination for what a hermit might be in the post-apocalypse

**Example:**  
A Cursed hermit who survived alone in a thawing Artic outpost and is fraught with visions.

### Outlander
Pretty much anyone who is good at navigating the post apocalypse or strange areas would fit this description already.

**Example:**  
A Starfolk who was the sole survivor of an island community destroyed by storms, and has a sixth sense for the strange weather and Flux phenomena of the world. 
### Sage
Teachers, weapons engineers, chemists, doctors, mathemeticians, and all sorts of nerdy occupations are still necessary in the post apocalypse, if only so that we can keep using all the cool stuff the Old World left behind. 

**Example:**  
A human wizard hunting scraps of theory trying to prove the Flux can be modeled mathematically.

### Soldier
Former U.S. Army remnant group, settlement guard, merchant convoy escort, bunker security and pretty much anyone who uses a gun for a job would fit this bill.

**Example:**  
A veteran of a war between emerging local factions who struck off on her own, but is having trouble reconciling what she did in the war. 

### Urchin
Any kid who commits crimes is an Urchin, I don't make the rules.

**Example:**  
A Wireling who grew up slipping in a bombed city and squeezed through rebar mazes to steal batteries from traffic systems.

### Noble
Old celebrities and people who are at the top of various post apocalypse social hierarchies and have somehow avoided the worst of the world.

**Example:**  
A survivor from a luxury bunker who uses their abundance of resources to control a local settlement.
