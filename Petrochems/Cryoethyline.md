Cryoethyline was developed by the [[USSR]] as a part of their Cold Ware "Siege Tactics" to strengthen infrastructure and production in anticipation of a Hot War with the [United States](United%20States). This technology allowed them to more efficiently colonize the [Artic Region](Artic%20Region).

Cryoethyline doubles as a renewable, highly efficient fuel, made from ethylene from potatoes and absorbed atmospheric carbon. as well as a highly effective industrial coolant. 

Cryoethyline is used in cryogenic applications, cold weather environments, and anywhere where efficiency beats power but Diesel doesn't cut it.
